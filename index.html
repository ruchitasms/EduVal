This tool is designed to study the gap between market hype and audited reality. We use institutional valuation metrics to determine if a stock's 2026 momentum is backed by its 2025 profitability.

Identify Value: Use EV/EBITDA to see the real price of cash flow. Lower = cheaper.
Monitor Risk: Use Net Debt/EBITDA to see how many years of profit it takes to pay debt.
Relative Valuation: Comparing a company's value to its peers to see if it's fair or expensive.
The Verdict Logic:
  • Value Play (< 10x): You are paying less than $10 for every $1 of annual cash profit (EBITDA). Often found in "mature" sectors like Energy (XOM, CVX) or Banking. These companies aren't expected to double their size overnight, so their "price tag" is lower relative to their earnings.
  • Fair Value (10x - 25x): This is the "Standard" range for healthy, established companies. Most blue-chip stocks like MSFT or GOOGL often sit in or near this range. It suggests a balance between current earnings and future growth expectations.
  • Premium Growth (> 25x): The market is willing to pay a high "premium" (over $25 for every $1 of current profit). This is typical for High-Tech (NVDA) or Disruptive (TSLA) companies. Investors pay more now because they expect the EBITDA to explode in the future.
